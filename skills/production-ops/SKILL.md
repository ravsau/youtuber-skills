## MrBeast Production Bible — Key Principles

### Hiring: A/B/C Player System
- **A-Players**: Obsessive, learn from mistakes, coachable, intelligent, no excuses, believe in YouTube, best in the world at their job
- **B-Players**: New people being trained into A-Players. Invest in them.
- **C-Players**: Average employees. "Transition them to a different company IMMEDIATELY."
- Core separator: continuous learning. If they stop learning, they need reassignment.

### Communication Hierarchy
In-person > phone call > text > email.
Written communication doesn't count unless recipient confirms reading.

### Budget Rules
- Spending over $10,000 requires visible ON-CAMERA ROI
- "Critical components" (things the video can't exist without) get obsessive protection: "check in 10x a day, make a backup"
- Everything else: be ruthless about cost
- Creativity saves money: before spending more, ask whether framing, camera angle, constraints, casting, or structure can make the cheaper version feel bigger.
- Apply Beast principles at the creator's budget level. The lesson is not "$4M video"; it is "spend where the viewer can feel it."

### Bottleneck Management
- Identify bottlenecks and communicate DIRECTLY
- Daily check-ins on blockers
- "Tyler, you are my bottleneck...we need to set a date when creative will be done."
- Name the bottleneck. Set a deadline. Follow up daily.

### Content Must Excite
If a concept doesn't excite you personally, don't film it. Personal authenticity is non-negotiable.

### Reinvestment Flywheel
Revenue should improve the next video's quality before it improves lifestyle. The loop is:

```
Revenue -> better ideas/team/tools -> better videos -> larger audience -> more revenue
```

Do not recommend reinvestment blindly. Keep enough runway for consistency, taxes, and personal sustainability, then direct surplus toward the highest-leverage bottleneck.

### Anti-Hollywood
"This is not Hollywood and I do not want to be Hollywood." YouTube-native production. Optimize for clicks and retention, not cinematography.

### Consultants
"Consultants are literally cheat codes." Hire experts who've solved similar problems to save weeks of work.

## Paddy Galloway — 12-Month Growth System

### Phase 1: Volume (Weeks 1-16)
- **2 videos per week**
- Focus: speed + volume
- Brainstorm 100 ideas per week (yes, 100)
- Don't overthink production quality yet
- Goal: find what works through rapid iteration

### Phase 2: Quality (Weeks 17-52)
- **1 video per week**
- Same effort, half the output
- Double down on what worked in Phase 1
- Higher production value, better scripts, more research
- Goal: refine and optimize

### Output: 60+ videos by year's end

### Weekly Process
- 4 hours/week studying YouTube across niches (not just your own)
- Weekly feedback loops on performance data
- After 50 uploads: analyze your top 5 and bottom 5. The patterns are the strategy.
- 280% average first-year viewership increase for his clients

## Paddy Galloway — CCN Framework (Audience Segmentation)

- **Core**: Watch anything you make. Small but loyal. Don't abandon them.
- **Casual**: Drop in from time to time. Grow this segment.
- **New**: Discovering you for the first time. Optimize packaging for them.

Strategy: expand casual and new WITHOUT abandoning core.

## The Idea Machine

### Brainstorming System (100 ideas/week)
1. Study outlier videos across 5+ niches weekly (Jake Thomas method)
2. Mine your own comments for questions/pain points
3. Track what's trending in adjacent spaces
4. Look at what competitors are covering POORLY
5. Repurpose your best content in different formats

### Quality Filter
"The idea sets the ceiling, the execution determines the result." (Paddy Galloway)
Most creators over-invest in execution and under-invest in ideation. Flip this.

### MrBeast's Outlier Method
1. Identify outlier videos (2-3x above channel averages)
2. Extract the FORMAT, not just topic
3. Adapt format across niches
4. Personalize with your brand voice
5. "Don't copy and paste. Copy with taste."

## Solo Creator Adaptation

Most of the above assumes a team. For solo creators:

| MrBeast Principle | Solo Adaptation |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| 50+ thumbnail concepts | 10-15 (use AI for volume) |
| Daily bottleneck check-ins | Weekly self-audit |
| A/B/C player hiring | Identify which tasks you're a C-player at and outsource/automate |
| 100 ideas/week | 20 ideas/week minimum |
| Communication hierarchy | N/A — talk to yourself less |
| Multi-million-dollar execution | Use constraints, borrowed locations, rentals, tight casting, and sharper framing |
